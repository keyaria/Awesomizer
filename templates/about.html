About the Awesomizer

The Awesomizer is a project brought to you by student developers of the Shapiro Design Lab. We wanted to show and share the books that everyone is reading – books that you think are awesome! Here’s how it works: you scan the book’s barcode, your vote gets uploaded to our site, and we tell you which books you think are the most awesome or worth sharing! For more information on the Shapiro Design Lab visit our website.
What is the AwesomeBox/Awesomizer?

This project originated from the Harvard Library Innovation Lab. You can learn more about Harvard’s version of the AwesomeBox here.

What makes something 'awesome'?

It could be exceptionally fun, unusual, helpful, look interesting or otherwise be amazing. You decide what is Awesome. By scanning your item it is displayed, anonymously, on a webpage of other Awesome University of Michigan Library items. You make it awesome.

Can I create my own Awesomebox at my library?

You sure can! Code for our project can be found on Github. For this project, we use a Raspberry Pi, a barcode scanner, and a computer. Our version of the project uses python for our barcode and data processing scripts, as well as SocketIO Flask for the server of this website. The scripts and code for this website can be found in our awesomizer repository.
