Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ):


What is The Awesomizer? Who made it?

The Awesomizer is a library engagement projet implemented by the student developers of the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor Shapiro Design Lab. The Shapiro Design Lab is a workshop located within the 1st floor of the UGLi, which houses tools, and consulting for students, etc. The Awesomizer was created to amalgamate a collection of books that everyone is reading -- books that are deemed awesome by readers.

How do I use The Awesomizer + Awesomebox?

Here's how it works:

  • Check out a book or find a book in the library.
  • If you think it was awesome, either:
    1. Deposit the book into an Awesomebox when you return it.
    2. Scan the book at an Awesomizer kiosk.
  • Check the recently awesomized list. Your book should appear.
  • Check out the other books - either most popylar or ones recently enjoyed by other students.
  • Repeat!

What makes something 'awesome'?

You. It could be exceptionally fun, unusual, helpful, look interesting or otherwise be amazing. You decide what is Awesome. By scanning your item it is displayed, anonymously, on a webpage of other awesome University of Michigan Library items. You make it awesome.

Where can I find an Awesomebox on campus?

Nowhere... yet! Our first physical Awesome Box is in the works and will be housed on the 1st floor of the Shapiro Undergraduate Library.

How was this project made?

Code for our project can be found on Github. For this project, we used a Raspberry Pi, a barcode scanner, and a computer. Our version of the project uses python for our barcode and data processing scripts, as well as SocketIO Flask for the server of this website. The scripts and code for this website can be found in our repository.
